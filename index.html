What Zedot stands for — resilience, quality without compromise, and the unwavering belief that African-built technology can lead on any global stage. Our ethos is not decorative. It is structural. It is the operating system behind every decision we make.

1. Grit

Companies like NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory and SpaceX have demonstrated that the most mission-critical systems are built by teams who refuse to accept failure as a terminal state. At Zedot, grit means staying in a failing CI/CD pipeline at 2am until it resolves — not because of obligation, but because the work demands it.

See this in practice

Research from McKinsey (2023) shows that engineering teams with high persistence scores deliver projects 34% closer to original scope than those without. We don't exit when it gets hard. We recalibrate and continue.

2. Resilience

The 2021 Fastly CDN outage took down 85% of the internet for an hour — teams without resilient fallback architectures had no playbook. Resilient engineering means designing for failure before failure designs your crisis response.

See this in practice

We build redundancy, runbooks, and recovery paths into every system we ship. Setbacks are data. We come back with better answers.

3. Security

The IBM Cost of a Data Breach Report 2024 places the global average breach cost at $4.88 million. The Zedot position: security is never a sprint-six task.

See this in practice

Every pull request, every API endpoint, every data pipeline we touch is evaluated against the OWASP Top 10 and relevant compliance frameworks from day one. Security is a first principle, not a retrofit.

4. Transparency

A 2022 PMI Pulse of the Profession report found that 37% of project failures stem from inaccurate status reporting. Zedot enforces honest project communication — sprint reviews, blocker escalations, and delivery forecasts that reflect reality.

See this in practice

Our clients always know where their project stands. No spin. No hidden pivots. No surprises on go-live day.

5. Compliance

Nigeria's NDPR (2019) and the EU's GDPR impose legal obligations on any organisation handling personal data — including fines of up to 4% of global annual turnover for violations.

See this in practice

We architect systems that are compliant by design — data residency, consent management, audit logging, and access controls are not bolted on. They are baked in.

6. Customer Centricity

Nielsen Norman Group research consistently shows that investing in user research before development reduces post-launch rework by up to 50%.

See this in practice

At Zedot, every architecture decision, API design, and UX flow is traced back to the person at the end of the product. We build for humans first. Technical elegance is a means, not the mission.

7. Modern & Agile

The 2024 State of Agile Report found that 71% of organisations using agile practices report faster time-to-market.

See this in practice

We run structured two-week sprints, maintain living backlogs, and adapt to stakeholder feedback without losing momentum or scope integrity. Agile is not a label we wear — it is the cadence we keep.

8. AI-First Mindset

GitHub's 2024 Octoverse report found that developers using AI coding assistance ship 55% faster on equivalent tasks. Zedot embeds ML tooling at every SDLC stage.

See this in practice

We believe in the transformative, near-endless possibilities of artificial intelligence — not as a buzzword, but as a daily practice embedded into every engagement.
